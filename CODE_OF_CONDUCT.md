Community code of conduct

This Code of Conduct defines shared expectations for behavior to keep our community welcoming, safe, and constructive for everyone. It applies across all spaces where the project is present: repositories, issues, discussions, pull requests, documentation, community channels, events, and any direct communications related to project work.

---

Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in this project and community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation. We commit to building a culture of empathy, respect, and accountability.

---

Our standards

Positive behavior

- Inclusive language: Use welcoming, inclusive language and be mindful of how your words land on others.
- Respect: Be respectful of differing viewpoints, experiences, and work styles.
- Constructive feedback: Offer critique that is specific, actionable, and kind; focus on ideas, not individuals.
- Collaboration: Assume good intent, ask clarifying questions, and seek mutual understanding.
- Support: Show empathy and encourage participation, especially from newcomers and underrepresented voices.
- Professionalism: Keep discussions on-topic, follow repository guidelines, and meet commitments or communicate risks early.

Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment: Any form of harassment, discrimination, or hate speech.
- Personal attacks: Insults, derogatory comments, or sustained disruption of conversations.
- Sexualized content: Sexual language or imagery, unwelcome advances, or attention.
- Trolling: Deliberate provocation, baiting, or inflammatory political/ideological rhetoric unrelated to project goals.
- Doxing and threats: Publishing private information, threats of violence, or intimidation.
- Unethical conduct: Encouraging illegal activity, exploiting vulnerabilities, or sabotaging community resources.

---

Reporting and support

- Where to report: Report concerns privately to the maintainers via the designated channel (e.g., conduct@project.org or a confidential form). If unsure, contact any maintainer to be directed appropriately.
- Confidentiality: Reports will be handled promptly and confidentially. We will not publicly name reporters without explicit consent.
- Information to include: Provide a description of the incident, where it occurred, names or handles of individuals involved, any witnesses, and any relevant links or screenshots.
- Immediate safety: If anyone is in immediate danger, contact local emergency services before notifying maintainers.

---

Enforcement guidelines

When a report is received, maintainers will follow these guidelines to determine consequences proportionate to the behavior and impact.

1. Correction
   - Community impact: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome but with limited impact.
   - Consequence: A private, written warning and clarification of expectations.

2. Warning
   - Community impact: A single incident or pattern of behavior that negatively affects participants or disrupts collaboration.
   - Consequence: A formal warning with clear expectations and consequences, potentially including moderation of interactions.

3. Temporary suspension
   - Community impact: Continued inappropriate behavior after warnings or serious violations.
   - Consequence: Temporary restriction from participation (e.g., commenting, committing, joining meetings) for a defined period.

4. Permanent suspension
   - Community impact: Demonstrated pattern of harmful behavior, harassment, or actions undermining community safety and trust.
   - Consequence: Permanent ban from project spaces and activities.

- Appeals: Individuals may appeal decisions by submitting a concise, factual statement. Maintainers will review appeals with an additional moderator for impartiality.
- Consistency: Similar incidents should receive similar consequences. Maintainers will document enforcement decisions to ensure consistency over time.

---

Community expectations for maintainers

- Transparency: Document decisions affecting community safety (without disclosing private details) and publish aggregate summaries where feasible.
- Impartiality: Recuse from handling reports where there is a conflict of interest; involve neutral moderators when needed.
- Restorative approach: When appropriate, focus on learning and repair alongside consequences.
- Periodic review: Revisit this Code regularly to address gaps, evolving needs, and community feedback.

---

Attribution and adoption

This Code of Conduct draws inspiration from widely adopted open-source community practices and templates that emphasize proactive, enforceable standards and clear reporting procedures. Adopting and enforcing a code of conduct signals an inclusive environment and equips maintainers to address problems effectively.
