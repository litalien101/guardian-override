# Guardian Override Community Code of Conduct

This Code of Conduct sets clear expectations for how we treat each other. It applies in all project spaces: repositories, issues, discussions, pull requests, documentation, community channels, events, and any direct communications related to project work.

---

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in Guardian Override respectful and free from harassment. Everyone is welcome, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity or expression, experience level, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual orientation.  

We commit to building a culture of empathy, respect, and accountability.

---

## Our standards

### Positive behavior

- Use inclusive, welcoming language.
- Respect different viewpoints, experiences, and work styles.
- Give feedback that is specific, actionable, and kind. Focus on ideas, not people.
- Collaborate with good intent. Ask questions and seek understanding.
- Show empathy and encourage participation, especially from newcomers.
- Keep discussions on-topic, follow guidelines, and communicate risks early.

### Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, or hate speech.
- Personal attacks, insults, or repeated disruption.
- Sexual language, imagery, or unwanted attention.
- Trolling or inflammatory comments unrelated to project goals.
- Sharing private information, threats, or intimidation.
- Encouraging illegal activity, exploiting vulnerabilities, or damaging community resources.

## Reporting and support

- **How to report:** Send concerns privately to maintainers through the designated channel (e.g., conduct@guardianoverride.org or a confidential form). If unsure, contact any maintainer.
- **Confidentiality:** Reports are handled promptly and kept private. Reporters will not be named without consent.
- **What to include:** Describe the incident, where it happened, who was involved, any witnesses, and relevant links or screenshots.
- **Immediate safety:** If anyone is in danger, contact local emergency services first.

---

## Enforcement guidelines

When a report is received, maintainers will act based on the impact of the behavior.

1. **Correction**
   - *Impact:* Inappropriate language or behavior with limited effect.
   - *Consequence:* Private written warning and clarification of expectations.

2. **Warning**
   - *Impact:* A single incident or repeated behavior that disrupts collaboration.
   - *Consequence:* Formal warning with clear expectations and possible moderation.

3. **Temporary suspension**
   - *Impact:* Continued inappropriate behavior after warnings or serious violations.
   - *Consequence:* Temporary restriction from participation for a set period.

4. **Permanent suspension**
   - *Impact:* Ongoing harmful behavior, harassment, or actions that undermine safety and trust.
   - *Consequence:* Permanent ban from project spaces and activities.

- **Appeals:** Decisions may be appealed with a concise, factual statement. Appeals will be reviewed with an additional moderator.
- **Consistency:** Similar incidents will be handled in similar ways. Enforcement decisions will be documented.

---

## Expectations for maintainers

- **Transparency:** Document decisions affecting community safety and share summaries where possible.
- **Impartiality:** Step aside if there is a conflict of interest; involve neutral moderators when needed.
- **Restorative approach:** When possible, focus on learning and repair along with consequences.
- **Review:** Revisit this Code regularly to address gaps and community feedback.
